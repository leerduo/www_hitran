HITRANonline

The High-Resolution Transmission Molecular Absorption Database (HITRAN) is a compilation of spectroscopic parameters that a variety of computer codes use to predict and simulate the transmission and emission of light in the atmosphere. It is hosted and developed at the Atomic and Molecular Physics Division of the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics under the direction of Dr Laurence S. Rothman.

This site provides an online interface for querying the HITRAN database:

  • Line-by-line search
  • IR cross section search
  • UV cross section search
  • Aerosol refractive indices
  • Collision-induced absorption cross section search
  • Additional data
